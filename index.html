Research Interests

Sim-to-real learning in robotics:
    Simulation tools greatly shorten the distance between theory and real world experiment. These tools not only make the collection of large amount of data possible, but also create platforms for efficient verification of algorithms. A good sim-to-real robotics system should consider two sides: the detailed robot model bridging the difference between the real-world dynamics and the one in simulation, and the abstract high-level representation of the environment bridging the gap between the real-world perception and the one in simulation. My research goal is to build detailed robots in simulator and come up with algorithms (Imitation Learning, RL, etc.) trained in simulators which can be applied on real robots with less efforts possible.

Multi-agent collaborative systems:
    Single robot has limitations as capacity in speed, force, payload and sensor range, etc. Collaborative strategies expand these capacities and can help the team achieve complex tasks. My research interest is the decentralized consensus based on predictions of others' strategies and using only local observations and with less communication possible.


Control and planning for flying robots:
    Quadrotors aroused plenty research passion in recent years thanks to its ability to navigate in cluttered environments. Precise control methods have been developed and high-performance aggressive maneuvers abilities have been shown. I am fascinated by the simplicity of its mechanical structure together with the complexity and non-linearity within its dynamics. The flying robots expand greatly the reachability of other robot systems and has promising future in real world applications. My research interest on the flying robots is to use learning techniques to train flying robots as 'atheletes' who can achieve difficult maneuvers with satisfactory performances.

Projects and Research

Collaborative transportation using multiple UAVs


    Collaborative control strategies for multiple UAVs physically connected to a payload via sphere joints. Modeling in ROS gazebo environment real world UAVs (Asctec Hummingbird) based on open-source codes; Optimal polynomial trajectory generation; Collaborative distribution of payload accelerations using decentralized methods (average-consensus, multi-agent reinforcement learning, etc).


github link: Collaborative_Aerial_Transportation


Sim-to-real imitation learning, depth-semantics representation of the environment

    Using depth-semantic features as the representation of the environment and the input to end-to-end neural network for local obstacle avoidance in indoor navigation tasks. Comparison of different types of representations of environment in navigation tasks, especially when human is in the task, trying to find the most appropriate input to the neural network in terms of task completion efficiency and social courtesy. RGBD camera noise studied, modeled, and added to the image data set before training, alleviating online computational pressure compared to online filtering.
video: Sim to real imitation learning in robot navigation


Swarm coverage algorithm based on description function

    Decentralized coverage algorithm studied where each agent, without communication among them and with information left behind at the target field where it has covered, can affect the overall coverage task. After the modeling of the coverage capability of each agent, Each agent acts according to the local information left by all the other agents. Specifically, agents align their velocity according to the direction of the less covered point possible in their neighbourhood. Difficulty lies in the solutions to the dead-lock situations and the efficiency of the coverage.




State estimation and control for swarm UAVs

Using motion capture system to estimate the state of a swarm of UAVs, applying control to each one in the team using centralized structure. Tried and compared with existing method using broadcasting as communication methods between the central node and the swarm and ICP algorithm as state estimator.
